## Writing a Post — Step by Step

### Step 1 — Start a New Post

Tap **New Post**. You land in the editor.

Your post is a **canvas** — a blank page where you place pieces of content called **blocks**. Each block can be moved anywhere, resized, and styled independently. It works like Canva or Google Slides.

### Step 2 — Pick Your Layout

Tap **Layout** at the top of the editor.

| Layout | Best For |
|---|---|
| **Editorial** | Long stories, lots of writing, clean and classic |
| **Full Bleed** | When you have one stunning photo to lead with |
| **Split Screen** | Photo on one side, text on the other — bold opening |
| **Gallery Lead** | Start with a grid of photos, then your story follows |
| **Minimal** | Short posts, text-first, no big hero image |

You can change the layout at any time without losing your content.

### Step 3 — Pick Your Theme

Tap **Theme** to style the post. Each post on your blog can look completely different — that's intentional.

**Color Palette — the mood of this post:**

| Palette | Feels Like |
|---|---|
| Desert Dusk | Golden hour, warm sand, adventure |
| Arctic | Open skies, crisp air, wide open spaces |
| Jungle | Dense green, heat, lush and alive |
| Monochrome | Timeless, elegant, black and white |
| Sunset | Pink and orange warmth |
| Ocean | Coastal calm, deep blue |
| Night Market | Dark, moody, electric atmosphere |
| Sage | Earthy, soft, grounded |

**Font Pairing — the personality of your writing:**

| Pairing | Feels Like |
|---|---|
| Expedition | A travel magazine — classic and refined |
| Modern Trek | Sharp and contemporary |
| Handwritten | A personal journal — warm and intimate |
| Bold Story | Confident and strong |
| Minimal | Clean, quiet, no-frills |

### Step 4 — Add Blocks to Your Canvas

Tap **+** anywhere on the canvas to add a block. Choose the type from the menu that appears.

#### Text Block

Your main writing. Type directly into the block.

Select any text to see the toolbar:
- **B** Bold &nbsp;&nbsp; *I* Italic
- Font family picker
- Font size slider
- Text color picker
- Block background color
- Alignment — left, center, right, justified

#### Heading Block

Titles, chapter names, location names. Larger than body text. Same style options as text.

Use headings to break long posts into chapters — it makes your writing feel like a real publication.

#### Photo — Single

One photo, full width or resized.

- Tap the block → select from your camera roll or files
- Drag the corner handles to resize
- Tap and hold to drag it anywhere on the canvas
- Add an optional caption below

#### Photo Gallery

Two, three, or four photos in a grid.

- Tap each slot to upload a photo
- Tap any photo to swap it out
- Add captions per photo or leave them clean

#### Pull Quote

A big, styled quote — like in a magazine.

Best with one or two sentences — your most vivid line. It automatically styles large and centered, using your palette's accent color.

#### Divider

A visual line between sections. Helps readers follow along in longer posts.

### Step 5 — Move and Resize Things

**Move a block**
- Phone — tap and hold the block, drag up or down
- Laptop — hover the left edge to see the drag handle, then drag

**Resize a photo**
- Tap the photo — handles appear at corners and edges
- Drag any corner to resize proportionally
- Drag a side handle to change width or height independently

**Change how wide a text block is**
- Tap the block → tap the settings icon (gear)
- Full Width / Wide / Centered Column / Narrow Column
- Narrow feels like a book. Full Width feels immersive.

### Step 6 — Fill In Post Details

Tap the **Info** tab at the top.

- **Title** — the headline
- **Subtitle** — optional second line
- **Tags** — words that describe the post (example: Japan, Street Food, Rainy Season). Readers can filter your archive by tag.
- **Hero Image** — the thumbnail shown in your post list and in notification emails. If you're using Full Bleed, this is your opening photo.

### Step 7 — Save Your Draft

The editor **auto-saves every 30 seconds**. You'll see "Draft saved" at the top of the screen.

Tap **Save Draft** anytime to save immediately.

If you close the browser or lose connection — your work is safe. Open the dashboard and your draft is right where you left it.

### Step 8 — Preview

Tap **Preview** to see exactly how readers will see your post — nothing is published yet. Toggle between mobile and desktop view in the preview.

### Step 9 — Publish

When you're ready:

1. Tap **Publish**
2. A screen shows how many subscribers will be notified and a preview of the email they'll receive
3. Tap **Confirm & Publish**

Your post is now live. Every subscriber gets an email with your hero image, post title, opening lines, and a link to read the full story.

## Editing a Published Post

1. Go to your post list
2. Tap the published post
3. Make changes — text, photos, theme, anything
4. Tap **Update Post**

The live post updates immediately. **Subscribers are not re-notified** when you edit — only on first publish.

## Your Subscribers

Readers sign up from your homepage with their name and email. They receive a confirmation email first — only after clicking confirm are they added to your list. This keeps your emails out of spam folders.

See your subscriber count in **Settings → Subscribers**.

## Tips

**Photos:**
- Landscape (wide) shots work best for Full Bleed and Split Screen
- Portrait (tall) shots work well as single photo blocks in Editorial
- Upload your original full-quality photo — the platform handles optimization

**Pull Quotes:**
- Use your single most vivid or surprising sentence
- One or two sentences maximum
- Should make someone want to read the whole post if they saw it alone

**Long posts:**
- Break them into chapters with Heading blocks
- Mix writing blocks with photo blocks rather than putting all photos at the end
- Use Dividers between major scene changes

**Short posts are completely valid:**
- Three paragraphs and five photos is a great post
- You don't need to write an essay every time

## If Something Isn't Working

**Editor feels slow or frozen**
Close the browser tab, wait 10 seconds, reopen. Your draft is auto-saved — nothing is lost.

**Photo won't upload**
Check your connection. Large photos on slow wifi can time out. Try again, or slightly compress the photo before uploading.

**Published post not showing on the blog**
Hard refresh the blog page (hold Shift and tap refresh on desktop). Should appear within seconds of publishing.

**Subscriber emails not sending**
This is a backend issue — contact the person who set up your blog.

**Want to change bio, profile photo, or blog name**
Bio and profile photo: go to **Settings** — editable directly.
Blog name and tagline: contact the person who set up your blog — takes about 10 minutes.

**Forgot your password**
Contact the person who set up your blog — they can reset it in about 5 minutes.
